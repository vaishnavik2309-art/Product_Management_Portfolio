# Perplexity AI UX Evaluation

## Overview
Evaluated Perplexity AI's citation-first interface to understand how its design builds user trust, transparency, and explainability in AI-generated answers.

## What I Analyzed
- Citation experience
- Source verification flow
- Follow-up question design
- Information hierarchy
- Trust and transparency mechanisms
- Overall usability using Nielsen's Heuristics and UX Laws

## Key Findings
- Citations are prominently displayed, making information verification easy.
- Source links help users trust AI-generated responses.
- Follow-up questions encourage deeper exploration.
- Large numbers of citations can create visual clutter and choice overload.
- Users may struggle to identify the most important or reliable source.
- Some interactions lack guidance for first-time users.

## Recommendations
- Improve visual hierarchy for source credibility.
- Add clearer explanations for citations and source authority.
- Reduce information overload when many sources are shown.
- Preserve multiple follow-up options for easier exploration.
- Improve discoverability of trust-building features.

## Outcome
Identified strengths and usability gaps in Perplexity's trust-focused design and proposed improvements to enhance explainability and user confidence.
