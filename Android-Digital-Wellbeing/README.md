# Redesign Android Digital Wellbeing

## Problem Statement
Android Digital Wellbeing helps users track screen time, but it primarily presents usage data without explaining the reasons behind behavioral patterns, leading to low engagement and limited long-term habit change.

## Objective
Redesign Android Digital Wellbeing to help users understand the "why" behind their screen usage through behavioral insights, trigger awareness, and pattern recognition, ultimately encouraging healthier digital habits.

## User Pain Points
- Users see screen-time numbers but struggle to understand what drives their behavior.
- Weekly reports often create guilt instead of meaningful reflection.
- App timers and focus modes are frequently overridden.
- Users lack visibility into recurring triggers such as stress, boredom, or habit loops.
- Insights feel static and do not support long-term learning.

## Proposed Solutions
- Session Stories explaining how a screen-time session unfolded.
- Trigger Maps highlighting common causes of phone usage across different times of the day.
- Weekly Pattern Analysis to identify recurring behavioral trends.
- Override Insights explaining why usage limits are extended.
- Personalized recommendations and micro-interventions based on user behavior.

## Success Metrics
- Insight Return Rate (users revisiting behavioral insights week-over-week)
- Session Insight Open Rate
- Weekly Pattern View Rate
- Repeat Insight Sessions (DAU/WAU)
- Reduction in Late-Night Scrolling Duration
- Emotional Safety Score
- Insight Dismissal Rate
